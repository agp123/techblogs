However, there will be new jobs created, jobs where someone needs to be able to fix a robot if they were to break down, but if someone isn't as skilled in that field jobs might still be hard to come by.

Blog Post 3: Interview

Alma Potrero

Ive chosen to interview my mother for this blog post. One reason being is that she was born in 1969 so growing up for her, she wasnt surrounded by technology the same way that I am. Another reason is that she was born and raised in Mexico so even if technology was advancing the way that it is now the access she had/would've had wouldn't be the same as mine. She grew up without technology and only when she came into America did she have the access she didnt before. Lastly as time passed, she saw the way things have changed technologically speaking.

Alma: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Mom: I was around 15 or 16.

Alma:What was your favorite piece of technology when you were growing up?

Mom: My favroite piece was the computer. My paraents gave it to me because I, along with my siblings needed it for honework. Even though we all shared it, I felt like it was mainly mine since I was the one that used it the most.

Alma: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Mom: Its definitely gotten better and faster.

Alma: Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or hurt society?

Mom: To me it seems like its helped, theres been a lot of changes since I was a child and it still keeps changing.

Alma: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Mom: I hope someone finds a way to make you feel closer to family thats not with you. I left my family back in Mexico and I miss them a lot, it'd be nicer to feel close again.

Alma: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology

Mom: Slow down a little bit, enjoy living in the moment and enjoying growing up but also take advantage of your resources.

Alma: Whats been your least favorite thing about technology?

Mom: I dont like how people have gotten so invested in being on their phones or computer. Nobody goes outside anymore and enjoys the things around them.
